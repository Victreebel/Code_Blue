Clinical mapping (authoritative — implement exactly)

For each cause: which investigations apply, the WITH-ultrasound clue, the WITHOUT-ultrasound clue (no-US mode is a difficulty toggle), and interventions.


Hypovolemia — invests: IV/IO, blood draw, iStat, POCUS. With US: flat/collapsing IVC + empty hyperdynamic LV. Without US: bleed/trauma/GI history, low Hgb on iStat, rising lactate, response to bolus. Interventions: fluid bolus, blood products, hemorrhage control.
Hypoxia — invests: capnography, airway assessment, breath sounds. Diagnosis needs NO ultrasound: ETCO2, tube confirmation, bilateral breath sounds, response to O2. Interventions: secure/confirm airway, high-quality ventilation.
Acidosis (H+) — invests: iStat/VBG, capnography, history. Without US: VBG pH/bicarb, ETCO2 trend, history (DKA/sepsis/renal). Interventions: optimize ventilation, bicarb in select cases, treat underlying.
Hyperkalemia — invests: 12-lead ECG, iStat, history. ECG IS diagnostic here: peaked T → wide QRS → sine wave; plus iStat K+, renal/dialysis/crush history. Interventions: calcium, insulin+dextrose, bicarb, albuterol.
Hypokalemia — invests: ECG (U waves, flat T), iStat K+. Interventions: K repletion, magnesium.
Hypothermia — invests: core temp, ECG (Osborn J waves), history. Interventions: active rewarming, prolonged resuscitation.
Tension pneumothorax — exam-diagnosable; US confirms but does NOT gatekeep. Without US: absent UNILATERAL breath sounds, hard to bag / rising airway pressure, hyperresonance, late tracheal deviation, mechanism. With US: absent lung sliding, barcode sign, lung point. Interventions: needle decompression, finger thoracostomy.
Tamponade — HARD MODE (design centerpiece): With US: pericardial effusion + diastolic RV collapse (clean). Without US: JVD, muffled tones, narrow pulse pressure, history (malignancy/uremia/recent MI or procedure/trauma), and ultimately DIAGNOSIS OF EXCLUSION → empiric pericardiocentesis. ECG must NEVER complete this line — low voltage / electrical alternans is a weak hint at most. Intervention: pericardiocentesis. Without US, this row should only reach green via the exclusion-then-empiric path. (Tension pneumo and tamponade are deliberate mirror images: without US, pneumo keeps strong clues, tamponade loses its cleanest — preserve that asymmetry.)
Toxins — invests: med/history review, glucose, tox screen, ECG. Without US: toxidrome pattern, glucose (hypoglycemic agents), response to antidote, ECG (wide QRS/long QT). Interventions: specific antidote, bicarb for Na-channel, lipid emulsion.
Thrombosis — PE — invests: history, capnography, POCUS, ECG. With US: RV strain (D-sign), McConnell's, IVC plethora, DVT. Without US: risk factors, low ETCO2 despite good CPR, sudden PEA; ECG (S1Q3T3) is SUGGESTIVE not diagnostic. Interventions: consider thrombolytics, prolonged CPR.
Thrombosis — MI — invests: 12-lead ECG (largely post-ROSC), history, troponin. With US: regional wall-motion abnormality. Interventions: post-ROSC PCI/cath, antiplatelets.
